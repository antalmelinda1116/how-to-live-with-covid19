The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Pandemic

Pandemic is a new situation we have to face with together. It affects somehow everyone's life in this world. During these days we should support and help even more each other then before. This website was made for helping you to find easily official information about the Pandemic in Ireland.
I tried to collect the most important general information what can be useful for you.

What is Coronavirus?

COVID-19 is an illness that can affect your lungs and airways. It's caused by a virus called coronavirus. It has many variant already, some of them can cause a really serious health issue regardless of age. Between elders and people with chronic illnesses unfortunately many times it ends with death. It's similar to flu but the type of inflamation caused in your lungs is more serious.

Symptoms

  • A fever (high temperature - 38 degrees Celsius or above)
  • A new cough - this can be any kind of cough, not just dry
  • Shortness of breath or breathing difficulties
  • Loss or change to your sense of smell or taste â€“ this means you've noticed you cannot smell or taste anything, or things smell or taste different to normal

You may not have all of these symptoms. It can take up to 14 days for symptoms to show. They can be similar to symptoms of cold and flu.

What to do if you think you are infected?

If you have any symptoms of COVID-19, you should self-isolate and phone a GP immediately. Your GP will tell you if you need a free COVID-19 test and how to cure yourself at home. Other people in your household will need to restrict their movements too. Having a lots of rest and sleep, drinking water enough, avoiding unhealthy habits and taking some medicines usually help to cure the light symtoms at home.
If your symptoms getting worse (really high fever, getting increasingly short of breath) you might need medical help.

Testing Centers

If you have symptoms of COVID-19 you can get a free COVID-19 test for your GP's advice. You can get free COVID-19 test without symptons also Read more. There are many options to make a Coronavirus test around Ireland. You can choose the Government's services or private testing centers too. If you know when you will need a test you should book an appointment days before to make sure you will have the results on time. The waiting time to get the results can be different in any testing center.

Travelling into Ireland

If you are planning to travel into Ireland you should check the actual restrictions properly. The Irish Government requieres a negative or 'not detected' PCR test within 72 hours and filling out the Passenger Locator Form from anyone arriving to the country.
Arriving without any evidence for not being infected can have serious consequences. After your arrivar you must stay in mandatory quarantine during 14 days. Of course there are exceptions as always (business trip, medical purpose, truck drivers etc.)
Also Ireland has a unique hotel quarantine system for travellers arriving from sertain countries. It means you must have pre-payed and pre-booked accomodation for the next 14 days after your arrival.

Prevention

Prevention is the most important part of the fight against the Coronavirus.


Wash your hands well and often

Cover your face in public places

Keep social distancing (2m)

Limit your contact with others

Physical Health

Apart of prevention keeping your body healthy is one of the most important thing during any Pandemic situation. In case you were not living a healthy life before, it's the perfect time to quit unhealthy habits and increase your immune system. To reach it you can:

  • Doing regularly exercises.
  • You can join to any online sport classes.
  • Go for a long walk in your area without stops.
  • Eating more protein and vegetables instead of carbohydrates which feeds diseased cells.
  • Sleeping regularly.
  • Drinking water enough.
  • Quit consuming alcohol, drugs or smoking.

Mental Health

Pandemic might cause a serious mental issue. To avoiding it you should try new indoor activities and see the Pandemic from a different point of view. Here I have some advices for you:

  • Keep doing your daily habits (included dressing up).
  • Doing exercises can keep not only physical but mental health too.
  • Set a limit to using social media and reading worrying news.
  • Keep in touch online with friends and communities you are part of.
  • In your extra free time start doing/learning something yoy always wanted.
  • Find relaxing habits.

In case you feel stressed, anxiety, useless, becoming irritable more easily, having sleeping troubles, etc. you should get professional help.
