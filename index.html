Symptoms

  • a fever (high temperature - 38 degrees Celsius or above)
  • a new cough - this can be any kind of cough, not just dry
  • shortness of breath or breathing difficulties
  • loss or change to your sense of smell or taste â€“ this means you've noticed you cannot smell or taste anything, or things smell or taste different to normal

You may not have all of these symptoms. It can take up to 14 days for symptoms to show. They can be similar to symptoms of cold and flu.

What to do if you think you are infected?

If you have any symptoms of COVID-19 (Coronavirus), you should self-isolate (stay in your room) and phone a GP immediately. Your GP will tell you if you need a free COVID-19 test and how to cure yourself at home. Other people in your household will need to restrict their movements too(stay at home). Having a lots of rest and sleep, drinking water enough, avoiding unhealthy habits (smoking, drinking alcohol) and taking some vitamins and medicines (Antibiotics, Ibuprofen) you can cure the light symtoms at home. If your symptoms getting worse (really high fever, getting increasingly short of breath) you might need medical help.

Vaccination

Fortunatley the Covid vaccine is already invented and We have many different options to be protected from serious symptons. There are a few suppliers such as Pfizer/BioNTech, Moderna, AstraZeneca, Sputnik V, Sinopharm etc. The vaccine is therefore a safe and clever way to produce an immune response in the body, without causing illness. In Europe getting the Covid vaccine is not obligatory but recommended.
